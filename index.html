Healthcare Accessibility and Population Service Gaps in Marsabit County

A GIS based spatial analysis project exploring healthcare accessibility and underserved regions across Marsabit County Kenya.

About the Project

Study Area

The study focuses on Marsabit County, located in the northern part of Kenya. The county covers a vast semi-arid landscape characterized by scattered settlements, limited infrastructure, and diverse ecological zones including forests, wetlands, and protected areas. Marsabit County has a relatively low population density, with many communities living in remote rural areas where access to essential services such as healthcare can be challenging. The county is connected by a network of major and minor roads linking towns and settlements, though large distances between facilities often affect service accessibility. Due to these characteristics, Marsabit provides a suitable case study for analyzing healthcare accessibility population distribution, and environmental constraints using GIS. The spatial analysis helps highlight areas with limited healthcare coverage and supports planning for improved service delivery across the region.

Objectives

General objectives

  • To analyze the spatial distribution and accessibility of healthcare facilities in Marsabit County using GIS in order to identify service gaps and support better healthcare planning.

    • Specific Objectives

  • To map the spatial distribution of health facilities across Marsabit County.
  • To analyze the population distribution in relation to existing health facilities.
  • To evaluate healthcare service coverage using buffer analysis around existing facilities.
  • To identify populated areas with limited access to healthcare services.
  • To determine suitable locations for potential new health facilities while considering road accessibility and environmental constraints such as protected areas, forests, and wetlands

GIS in Healthcare

GIS in Healthcare Geographic Information Systems (GIS) play an important role in improving healthcare planning and service delivery by enabling the collection, analysis, and visualization of spatial data related to health services and populations. GIS helps decision-makers understand how healthcare facilities are distributed across geographic areas and how easily communities can access these services. In healthcare planning, GIS can be used to map the locations of hospitals, health centers, and dispensaries, analyze their proximity to population centers, and evaluate accessibility through transportation networks such as roads. This allows planners to identify areas with insufficient healthcare coverage and prioritize locations where new health facilities may be needed. GIS is also useful for public health monitoring, disease surveillance, emergency response, and resource allocation. By integrating different datasets such as population distribution, environmental factors, and infrastructure networks, GIS provides valuable insights that support evidence-based decision-making in the health sector. Overall, GIS enhances the ability of governments, healthcare organizations, and planners to improve service delivery, reduce spatial inequalities in healthcare access, and support sustainable health infrastructure development.

Spatial Analysis Insights

Population Distribution

This population distribution map illustrates how people are spatially distributed within Marsabit County, providing an important foundation for analyzing health service accessibility in the region. The map shows that population clusters are mainly concentrated around major towns, represented by the orange points, while large parts of the county remain sparsely populated. These settlement patterns suggest that many residents live near key service centers where infrastructure and public facilities, including health services, are more likely to be available. The higher population concentrations around towns highlight areas with potentially greater demand for healthcare facilities such as hospitals, clinics, and dispensaries. However, the map also reveals extensive low-density areas across the county, which may experience limited access to healthcare due to long travel distances and poor infrastructure. In a large and predominantly arid county like Marsabit, dispersed settlements and challenging terrain can significantly affect the ability of residents to reach medical services in a timely manner. Therefore, understanding the spatial relationship between population distribution and settlement centers is essential for identifying underserved areas, guiding the placement of new health facilities, and improving overall healthcare accessibility within the county.

Facility Coverage

This map shows the spatial distribution of health facilities across Marsabit County, forming a key component of the health accessibility analysis in the study area. Different symbols represent various types of health facilities, including dispensaries, health centres, district hospitals, clinics, and laboratories. The map also includes towns and major road networks, which are important for understanding how residents travel to access healthcare services. Most facilities appear to be concentrated around major towns and along the main transport corridors, indicating that infrastructure and settlement patterns strongly influence where health services are located. However, the distribution also reveals that several parts of Marsabit County, particularly remote and sparsely populated areas, have fewer health facilities. This uneven distribution suggests that some communities may need to travel long distances to reach essential healthcare services, which can limit timely medical care. In the context of the health accessibility project, this map helps identify potential service gaps and underserved regions, providing valuable information for planning the placement of new facilities, improving road connectivity, and ensuring more equitable access to healthcare across the county.

Environmental Constraints

This map illustrates the major environmental factors that may influence health accessibility across Marsabit County. It highlights key environmental features such as forests, protected areas, and wetlands alongside towns and the road network. These features are important in the health accessibility project because they can act as natural constraints that affect the movement of people and the placement of health facilities. For example, wetlands and protected areas may limit road construction or slow travel, while forested regions and conservation zones may restrict infrastructure development. The map therefore helps to identify areas where environmental conditions could influence how easily residents can reach healthcare services. In the context of the project, understanding these environmental constraints is essential for planning equitable healthcare access across the county. Some settlements located near wetlands or within protected landscapes may experience difficulties reaching nearby health facilities due to limited transportation routes or environmental restrictions. By integrating environmental features with settlement locations and road networks, the map supports the identification of areas where healthcare accessibility may be reduced. This information can guide planners and decision-makers in improving road connectivity, selecting appropriate sites for future health facilities, and ensuring that environmental conservation and healthcare access are balanced within Marsabit County.

Project Methodology

Data Collection

The data used in this study were obtained from secondary sources, primarily from online geospatial data repositories and official datasets. The collected datasets included health facility locations, population distribution, road networks, towns, protected areas, forests, and wetlands within Marsabit County. These datasets were acquired in spatial formats compatible with Geographic Information Systems (GIS). The data were selected based on their relevance to analyzing healthcare accessibility and spatial distribution of services within the study area.

Data Processing

Following data acquisition, preprocessing procedures were carried out to ensure data quality and consistency. This involved data cleaning, attribute verification, and removal of incomplete or redundant records. Additionally, all datasets were reprojected to a common Coordinate Reference System (CRS) to ensure spatial alignment during analysis. The datasets were also clipped to the administrative boundary of Marsabit County in order to focus the analysis exclusively on the study area and eliminate unnecessary spatial data outside the region.

Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis techniques were applied to evaluate the distribution and accessibility of healthcare services across the county. Buffer analysis was conducted around existing health facilities to determine their service coverage areas and identify populations located within or beyond these zones. Overlay analysis was used to integrate population data with healthcare facility locations in order to assess accessibility patterns. Environmental layers such as protected areas, forests, and wetlands were also incorporated to evaluate potential constraints when identifying suitable locations for additional healthcare facilities. These spatial analyses provided insights into areas that are underserved by existing healthcare infrastructure.

Visualization

The results of the spatial analysis were communicated through a series of thematic maps and graphical representations. Maps were produced to illustrate the distribution of health facilities, population density, transportation networks, environmental constraints, and healthcare service coverage. In addition, charts such as pie charts and distribution graphs were generated to present the composition and spatial distribution of different types of healthcare facilities. These visualizations enhance the interpretation of spatial patterns and support effective communication of the study findings.

Key Recommendations

Based on the spatial analysis of healthcare accessibility and population distribution in Marsabit County, the following recommendations are proposed:

Establishment of Additional Health Facilities

The analysis indicates that several areas in Marsabit County fall outside the 10 km service coverage of existing health facilities. It is recommended that new health facilities be established in underserved areas to improve healthcare accessibility for the local population.

Improvement of Road Infrastructure

Limited road connectivity can reduce access to healthcare services even when facilities exist. Improving the transportation network will enhance movement of patients and healthcare providers, especially in remote settlements.

Consideration of Environmental Constraints

Future health infrastructure development should take into account protected areas, forests, and wetlands to ensure environmentally sustainable development and avoid ecological conflicts.

Use of GIS for Health Planning

County governments and health planners should adopt Geographic Information Systems (GIS) as a decision-support tool for planning the location of future health facilities, monitoring service coverage, and identifying underserved populations.
